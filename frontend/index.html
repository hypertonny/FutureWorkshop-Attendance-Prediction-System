    Model Q&A (Context from Streamlit)

    Model Types and Why They Were Chosen

    Logistic Regression: linear baseline, fast, interpretable, and validates whether feature engineering has strong signal.

    Random Forest: bagging ensemble for non-linear patterns and reduced variance through parallel trees.

    XGBoost: boosting ensemble for sequential error correction and stronger bias reduction on structured data.

    These three represent linear, bagging, and boosting families, covering the core classifier spectrum without unnecessary model sprawl.

    Threshold Optimization and Metrics Explained

    The model outputs probabilities, then a threshold converts them into attend or no-show predictions.

    Instead of fixed 0.5, threshold is optimized using F1 score so precision and recall stay balanced for planning decisions.

    F1 is primary because attendance data can be imbalanced. AUC-ROC measures ranking quality, while raw accuracy alone can be misleading.

    Cross-validation and threshold sweep are used to reduce split luck and improve deployment reliability.

    Multicollinearity and Feature Strategy

    Engineered features may be correlated by design, especially derived indicators.

    Tree models tolerate this well and can down-weight noisy or redundant features. Logistic regression is kept mainly as a baseline rather than sole interpretability source.

    The strategy is to offer rich signal and let model families identify what matters most, then monitor drift through scheduled audits and retraining.
